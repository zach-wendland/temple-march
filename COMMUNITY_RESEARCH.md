## 1. COMMUNITY SENTIMENT ON EXISTING ORDER 66 DEPICTIONS

### Star Wars Battlefront II (2005) - "Operation: Knightfall"

**What Fans LOVE:**
- The iconic opening narration: *"What I remember about the rise of the Empire is... is how quiet it was."*
- The 501st Legion perspective showing moral complexity and reluctant soldiers
- Being able to play as Anakin alongside clone troopers
- The emotional weight of the mission's context

**What Fans HATE / Want Changed:**
- Notorious difficulty spike - considered one of the hardest missions in the game
- Frustrating "defend the bookcases" objective (protecting holocrons from Jedi)
- Infinitely spawning Jedi that overwhelm players
- Three Jedi Masters with regenerating health as a punishing final objective
- Basic Jedi AI that feels repetitive (block > jump > attack pattern)
- Instant-kill scenarios that feel unfair

**Source**: [Steam Community Discussions](https://steamcommunity.com/app/6060/discussions/0/141136086934135729/)

### Star Wars Battlefront II (2017) - DICE Version

**Community Disappointment:**
- **No Order 66 content at all** - Campaign focuses on post-Return of the Jedi era
- Fans frequently request Order 66 as DLC or expansion content
- The game is praised for visuals/audio but criticized for missing this iconic era

**Source**: [TheGamer Comparison](https://www.thegamer.com/star-wars-battlefront-2-ea-reasons-best-battlefront-game/)

### Jedi: Fallen Order - Order 66 Flashback

**What Fans LOVE:**
- Emotional impact of Jaro Tapal's sacrifice protecting young Cal
- First-person immersion during the betrayal
- Made players genuinely care about the trauma
- Hidden Easter egg: Sidious saying "Execute Order 66" if you linger at a door

**What Could Be Expanded:**
- Fans note the Clone Wars animated series had more emotional weight because "we had a whole series getting to know the characters"
- Desire for deeper connection to the clones who betray you

**Source**: [ResetEra Discussion](https://www.resetera.com/threads/what-were-your-reactions-to-the-entrance-at-the-end-of-jedi-fallen-order-spoilers-obviously.231088/page-3), [Fandom Discussion](https://starwars.fandom.com/f/p/4400000000000057777)

### Star Wars: Episode III - Revenge of the Sith Video Game (2005)

**Fan Nostalgia & Remake Demand:**
- 231+ signatures on Change.org petition for remake/remaster
- Praised as "the best fighting game the franchise has ever seen"
- Featured Cin Drallig and Serra Keto as major boss fights
- Multiplayer duel mode remains beloved
- Players want: Modern graphics (Fallen Order quality), expanded content, online multiplayer

**Boss Fight Highlights:**
- Cin Drallig portrayed as strongest duelist in Jedi Order (trained by Yoda)
- Serra Keto (his padawan) as co-boss
- The game expanded on content only glimpsed in the film

**Source**: [DorksideOfTheForce](https://dorksideoftheforce.com/2023/05/31/star-wars-fans-demand-revenge-sith-game-remake/), [GameRant](https://gamerant.com/star-wars-episode-3-revenge-sith-20-year-anniversary-legacy/)

## 4. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Playing the Villain - Fan Sentiment

**Arguments FOR (Majority Position):**
- "Bad guys are 'cool.' Darth Vader is by far more popular than Luke Skywalker"
- Battlefront II 2005 proved it can work with proper narrative framing
- KOTOR series shows dark side content can be compelling and thought-provoking
- Force Unleashed's Starkiller was well-received as a dark protagonist
- The tragedy makes it MORE impactful, not less

**Arguments for Caution:**
- Some players report feeling "uncomfortable" with certain evil choices in SWTOR
- Players maintain moral lines even when playing villains
- One player: "There were a few times where I just couldn't bring myself to do it"

**Design Solution Observed:**
- SWTOR's moral dilemmas make players appreciate ethical warfare
- Grey decisions provide "an opportunity to stop and think: 'what would I do?'"
- Internal discussion is "important and beneficial for self-development"

**Source**: [ReligionDispatches SWTOR](https://religiondispatches.org/a-star-wars-videogame-invites-players-to-the-dark-side/), [ScreenRant Villain Games](https://screenrant.com/star-wars-game-sith-evil-dark-side-villain/)

### The Youngling Scene - Handling Dark Content

**Current Canon Approach:**
- The film shows Anakin igniting his saber, cuts away before violence
- Comics reveal Anakin heard the final thoughts of every youngling he killed
- His grief and self-loathing fueled further dark side descent
- The tragedy is the point - showing the depth of his fall

**Recommendations from Fan Discourse:**
1. **Implication over depiction** - Show the door, the saber igniting, then cut
2. **Emotional weight** - Make players feel the horror, not participate in it
3. **Narrative distancing** - Present as memory/flashback rather than active gameplay
4. **Player agency limitation** - This moment should NOT be interactive combat
5. **Audio design** - Use sound to convey tragedy without showing violence

**Precedent:**
- Obi-Wan Kenobi series showed Reva's perspective as a survivor
- "Handheld camera, single cut" created panic and immersion without graphic violence

**Source**: [ScreenRant Youngling Scene](https://screenrant.com/star-wars-made-anakin-skywalker-youngling-slaughter-more-disturbing/), [CBR Analysis](https://www.cbr.com/star-wars-revenge-sith-anakin-massacred-younglings/)

### Dismemberment & Violence Rating

**Fan Desire:**
- Many fans want human dismemberment for authentic lightsaber combat
- Jedi Knight series had dismemberment at T rating (no blood, just limbs)
- "Realizing the brutal ways a lightsaber can maim would earn an M rating... showing fans the team's determination not to have everything be kid-friendly"

**Industry Reality:**
- Disney prefers T rating for maximum reach
- Fallen Order limited dismemberment to droids/animals only
- Jedi: Survivor added human dismemberment (toggleable) while keeping T rating

**Compromise Solution:**
- Toggleable dismemberment option
- Focus on droids and non-humanoid enemies for most combat
- Reserve human dismemberment for significant story moments
- Use "scorched" kills rather than bloody ones

**Source**: [GamesRadar Dismemberment](https://www.gamesradar.com/star-wars-jedi-fallen-order-dismemberment/), [ScreenRant M Rating](https://screenrant.com/star-wars-jedi-survivor-game-lightsaber-combat-mature/)

## 6. TONE AND ATMOSPHERE

### Narrative Framing Preferences

**The Kreia Debate (KOTOR 2) - Villain Perspective:**
- Some fans want pure tragic framing with no redemption
- Others prefer moral complexity with "grey" decisions
- "She's kind of right about the Jedi being flawed" - nuanced villain philosophy
- Deconstruction can be enlightening when characters are well-written

**Consensus Approach:**
- Present Anakin as a tragic figure, not a triumphant villain
- Show his internal conflict and manipulation by Palpatine
- The player should feel the weight of his choices
- "Vader becomes a truly tragic figure, someone who failed and gave in to his basest desires, becoming a monster"

**Source**: [TV Tropes KOTOR 2](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/review_comments.php?id=13798), [Narrative First Star Wars](https://narrativefirst.com/blog/the-story-structure-of-the-original-star-wars-trilogy)

### Tragic vs Triumphant - The Verdict

**TRAGIC is the consensus:**
- This is a fall from grace, not a victory
- Anakin is losing his soul, not winning a battle
- The Jedi are victims, not just enemies to defeat
- Clone troopers are also victims of programming

**How to Execute:**
- Show Anakin's hesitation and pain
- Include moments where he recognizes former allies
- Let the player feel the wrongness of the action
- End with the hollow "victory" - everything is ashes

### Music and Sound Design Preferences

**John Williams Tracks to Reference/License:**
- "Anakin's Dark Deeds" - symbolizes the fall
- "Anakin's Betrayal" - choir-driven, tear-inducing
- "Order 66" theme - chilling and sad
- "The Immolation Scene" - tragic finality
- "Palpatine's Teachings" - ominous, dark side energy
- "Enter Lord Vader" - militaristic transformation

**Musical Techniques:**
- **Dies Irae melody** - used for darkest moments in saga (Sand People, Jedi slaughter)
- **Operatic approach** - the duel music and Order 66 sequence
- **Choir integration** - heightens emotional tragedy
- **Imperial March hints** - foreshadowing Vader's emergence

**Sound Design:**
- Lightsaber hum variations (Anakin's blue vs Temple Guards' yellow)
- Clone trooper comms chatter (militaristic, clinical)
- Distant blaster fire as battles rage elsewhere
- Silence in key moments (the "how quiet it was" narration)
- Jedi death cries and Force echoes

**Source**: [JWFan](https://jwfan.com/?p=10681), [TheForce.net Music](https://www.theforce.net/story/front/The_Music_Of_Star_Wars_A_Recollection_Episode_III_160277.asp)

## 7. LIGHTSABER COMBAT PREFERENCES

### What Fans Want from Combat

**Jedi Academy Model (Praised):**
- Lightsabers feel deadly - 1-2 hits kill most enemies
- "Lightsabers actually feel dangerous, just as much to you as to the enemy"
- Acrobatic freedom and skill variations
- Not about health bars, but positioning and timing

**Fallen Order/Survivor Model (Mixed):**
- "Feel like you're hitting stuff with a glowing stick"
- BUT praised for smoothness and responsiveness
- Survivor's stance system and dismemberment were upgrades

**Ideal Hybrid:**
- Jedi Academy/Force Unleashed's acrobatic power
- Fallen Order's smoothness and control
- Ghost of Tsushima's stance-switching system
- Seven lightsaber combat forms as selectable stances

## DESIGN PRIORITY MATRIX

### MUST HAVE (Core Experience)
1. Play as Anakin leading the Temple assault
2. Multiple Jedi enemy types (padawan, knight, master, Temple Guard)
3. Cin Drallig boss fight
4. Tragic narrative framing with emotional weight
5. John Williams-inspired score with Order 66/Dark Deeds themes
6. Holocron Vault/Archives location
7. 501st Legion presence with named clones

### SHOULD HAVE (Strong Desire)
1. Multiple Force powers (Choke, Push, Throw, etc.)
2. Combat stance/style system
3. Challenge mode with wave survival
4. Unlockable characters for replays
5. Easter eggs referencing Clone Wars, Rebels, games
6. Dismemberment (toggleable)
7. Co-op mission potential

### NICE TO HAVE (Enhancement)
1. Multiplayer duel mode (ala ROTS 2005)
2. Speedrun/score attack leaderboards
3. New Game+ with enhanced difficulty
4. Gallery mode with concept art/lore
5. Alternative "what if" ending (Anakin resists)
6. Playable clone trooper segments

### AVOID
1. Interactive youngling violence (imply, don't show)
2. Triumphant tone (this is a tragedy)
3. Making Jedi feel like generic enemies
4. Frustrating infinite spawns (BF2 2005's main complaint)
5. Health sponge enemies (lightsabers should feel deadly)
6. Shallow replayability
